Arthroscopy Knee, Shoulder & Ankle

Minimally Invasive Joint Surgeries for Improved Mobility

What is Arthroscopy?

Arthroscopy is a minimally invasive surgical procedure used to diagnose and treat joint problems. It involves the use of a small camera, called an arthroscope, which is inserted into the joint through a small incision. This allows the surgeon to view the inside of the joint on a screen and perform necessary repairs using specialized instruments.

Arthroscopy of the Knee
Common Conditions Treated:
  • Meniscal Tears: Repairing or removing torn meniscus cartilage.
  • Ligament Injuries: Reconstruction of torn ligaments like the ACL (Anterior Cruciate Ligament).
  • Cartilage Damage: Smoothing out or repairing damaged cartilage.
  • Inflammation: Removing inflamed synovial tissue.
  • Loose Bodies: Removing fragments of bone or cartilage that are floating within the joint.
Arthroscopy of the Shoulder
Common Conditions Treated:
  • Rotator Cuff Tears: Repairing tears in the rotator cuff tendons.
  • Shoulder Impingement: Removing inflamed or damaged tissue to relieve impingement.
  • Labral Tears: Repairing or trimming the torn labrum.
  • Frozen Shoulder: Releasing tight joint capsule to improve range of motion.
  • Instability: Repairing torn or stretched ligaments to stabilize the shoulder.
Arthroscopy of the Ankle
Common Conditions Treated:
  • Ankle Instability: Repairing ligaments to stabilize the ankle.
  • Arthritis: Removing loose cartilage or bone fragments, and smoothing the joint surfaces.
  • Osteochondral Defects: Repairing damaged cartilage and underlying bone.
  • Synovitis: Removing inflamed synovial tissue.
  • Scar Tissue: Removing scar tissue that restricts movement.
Benefits of Arthroscopy
  • Minimally Invasive: Smaller incisions result in less tissue damage and scarring.
  • Faster Recovery: Patients typically experience quicker recovery times compared to traditional open surgery.
  • Less Pain: Reduced postoperative pain and discomfort.
  • Lower Risk of Infection: Smaller incisions reduce the risk of infection.
  • Outpatient Procedure: Most arthroscopic surgeries are performed on an outpatient basis, allowing patients to return home the same day.
Recovery

Recovery times vary depending on the joint treated and the specific procedure performed. Patients generally follow a rehabilitation program that includes physical therapy to restore strength and mobility. Your surgeon will provide detailed postoperative care instructions tailored to your specific needs.
