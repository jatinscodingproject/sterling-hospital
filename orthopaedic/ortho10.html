Rheumatoid Arthritis (RA)

At Sterling Orthopaedic Hospital, we recognize the impact of Rheumatoid Arthritis (RA) on our patients' lives. We provide comprehensive care and management strategies for this chronic condition.

What is Rheumatoid Arthritis (RA)?

Rheumatoid arthritis (RA) is a chronic inflammatory disorder that primarily affects the joints, but it can also impact other systems in the body, including the skin, eyes, heart, lungs, and blood vessels. Unlike the wear-and-tear damage of osteoarthritis, RA affects the lining of your joints, causing painful swelling that can eventually result in bone erosion and joint deformity.

Symptoms of Rheumatoid Arthritis
  • Joint Pain and Swelling: Persistent pain and swelling in the joints, particularly in the hands, wrists, and knees.
  • Stiffness: Morning stiffness lasting for 30 minutes or longer.
  • Fatigue: A feeling of tiredness and lack of energy.
  • Warmth and Redness: Inflamed joints may feel warm to the touch and appear red.
  • Loss of Function and Mobility: Reduced ability to move the affected joints.
Diagnosis of Rheumatoid Arthritis

Early diagnosis and treatment of RA are crucial to managing symptoms and preventing joint damage. Our comprehensive diagnostic approach includes:

  • Medical History and Physical Examination: A thorough assessment of symptoms, medical history, and physical examination of affected joints.
  • Laboratory Tests: Blood tests to check for markers of inflammation and specific antibodies (such as rheumatoid factor and anti-CCP) associated with RA.
  • Imaging Studies: X-rays, ultrasound, and MRI scans to assess joint damage and inflammation.
Treatment Options for Rheumatoid Arthritis

At Sterling Orthopaedic and IVF Centre, we offer a range of treatments to manage RA and improve quality of life.

Medications
  • Nonsteroidal Anti-Inflammatory Drugs (NSAIDs): Help reduce pain and inflammation.
  • Corticosteroids: Powerful anti-inflammatory drugs used to control severe symptoms.
  • Disease-Modifying Anti-Rheumatic Drugs (DMARDs): Slow the progression of RA and save joints and other tissues from permanent damage.
Physical Therapy

Our experienced physiotherapists work with patients to develop personalized exercise programs that help maintain joint flexibility and strength, reduce pain, and improve overall function.

Occupational Therapy

Occupational therapy focuses on helping patients perform daily activities more easily and safely. This may include learning new ways to complete tasks, using assistive devices, and modifying the home or work environment.

Surgical Options

In cases where joint damage is severe, surgical intervention may be necessary. We offer advanced surgical options, including:

  • Synovectomy: Removal of the inflamed synovial tissue.
  • Tendon Repair: Repair of damaged tendons around the joint.
  • Joint Fusion: Fusing two or more bones together to reduce pain.
  • Total Joint Replacement: Replacing the damaged joint with a prosthesis.
