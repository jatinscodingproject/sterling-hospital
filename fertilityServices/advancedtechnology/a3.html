Infertility Assessment Panel

An Infertility Assessment Panel is a thorough diagnostic evaluation designed to identify the underlying causes of infertility in individuals or couples who are struggling to conceive. This panel includes a series of tests and assessments aimed at providing a detailed understanding of both male and female reproductive health. By identifying specific issues, the panel helps in creating a tailored treatment plan to enhance the chances of successful conception.

Who Should Consider an Infertility Assessment Panel?
  • Couples Trying to Conceive: Couples who have been trying to conceive for over a year without success should consider undergoing an infertility assessment.
  • Individuals with Known Medical Conditions: Those with known reproductive health issues or chronic conditions that may impact fertility.
  • Women Over 35 or Men Over 40: Age-related fertility decline is more pronounced in women over 35 and men over 40, making an assessment crucial for optimizing treatment.
  • Patients with Previous Pregnancy Loss: Individuals or couples who have experienced recurrent miscarriages may benefit from a comprehensive fertility evaluation.
Components of the Infertility Assessment Panel
Medical History and Physical Examination

What It Involves: A detailed review of medical history, including previous pregnancies, surgeries, medications, and lifestyle factors. Physical examinations may be conducted to assess overall health and reproductive organs.

Hormonal Assessments
  • For Women: Tests may include measuring levels of hormones such as Follicle Stimulating Hormone (FSH), Luteinizing Hormone (LH), Estradiol, Progesterone, Thyroid Hormones, and AMH (Anti-Mullerian Hormone) to evaluate ovarian reserve and overall hormonal balance.
  • For Men: Hormonal tests may include levels of Testosterone, Luteinizing Hormone (LH), and Follicle Stimulating Hormone (FSH) to assess hormonal influences on sperm production.
Semen Analysis

What It Involves: Evaluation of sperm count, motility, morphology, and overall sperm health to identify potential male fertility issues.

Imaging Studies
  • For Women: Ultrasound or hysterosalpingography (HSG) to assess the structure of the uterus and fallopian tubes, identify abnormalities, and evaluate tubal patency.
  • For Men: Scrotal ultrasound may be used to detect conditions such as varicocele or other structural issues.
Genetic Testing

For Both Partners: Genetic tests can identify chromosomal abnormalities or genetic conditions that may affect fertility or increase the risk of genetic disorders in offspring.

Endometrial Receptivity Testing

What It Involves: Tests like the Endometrial Receptivity Array (ERA) to evaluate the timing and receptivity of the endometrial lining for embryo implantation.

Additional Tests
  • Immunological Tests: To check for immune system issues that may impact fertility.
  • Infectious Disease Screening: To identify infections that could affect reproductive health.
Benefits of the Infertility Assessment Panel
  • Comprehensive Diagnosis: Provides a complete picture of both partners' reproductive health to identify specific causes of infertility.
  • Tailored Treatment Plans: Helps fertility specialists design personalized treatment strategies based on individual findings.
  • Informed Decision-Making: Equips couples with the knowledge needed to make informed decisions about their fertility treatment options.
