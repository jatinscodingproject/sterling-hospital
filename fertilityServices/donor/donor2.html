Donor Sperm IUI/IVF

Donor sperm Intrauterine Insemination (IUI) and In Vitro Fertilization (IVF) are fertility treatments that use sperm from a donor to help individuals and couples achieve pregnancy. These procedures are especially beneficial for those with male infertility issues and single women

What is Donor Sperm IUI?

Intrauterine Insemination (IUI) with donor sperm involves directly placing the donor sperm into the recipient's uterus around the time of ovulation, increasing the chances of fertilization.

What is Donor Sperm IVF?

In Vitro Fertilization (IVF) with donor sperm involves fertilizing the recipient's eggs with donor sperm in a laboratory setting. The resulting embryos are then transferred to the recipient's uterus.

Who Can Benefit from Donor Sperm IUI/IVF?

Donor Sperm IUI/IVF is an option for:

  • Couples with severe male infertility issues (e.g., low sperm count, poor sperm motility)
  • Single women who wish to conceive
  • Individuals or couples who carry genetic disorders they do not want to pass on to their offspring
The Donor Sperm IUI Process

Selection of Donor Sperm:

  • Recipients choose a sperm donor from a sperm bank. Donors are thoroughly screened for medical, genetic, and psychological health.

Ovulation Monitoring:

  • The recipient’s ovulation cycle is monitored to determine the optimal time for insemination.

Sperm Preparation:

  • Donor sperm is thawed and prepared in the lab to increase its motility.

Insemination:

  • The prepared sperm is inserted directly into the uterus using a thin catheter.
The Donor Sperm IVF Process

Selection of Donor Sperm:

  • Similar to IUI, recipients choose a sperm donor from a sperm bank.

Ovarian Stimulation:

  • The recipient undergoes hormonal treatment to stimulate the ovaries to produce multiple eggs.

Egg Retrieval:

  • Eggs are retrieved from the ovaries using a minor surgical procedure.

Fertilization and Embryo Culture:

  • The retrieved eggs are fertilized with donor sperm in the lab, and the embryos are cultured for several days.

Embryo Transfer:

  • The best quality embryos are selected and transferred to the recipient’s uterus.

Pregnancy Test:

  • A pregnancy test is conducted about two weeks after the embryo transfer.

Success Rates and Considerations

  • IUI: Success rates for IUI with donor sperm can vary depending on the recipient’s age, health, and fertility status. Generally, IUI is less invasive and less expensive but may require multiple attempts.
  • IVF: IVF typically has higher success rates compared to IUI, especially for women over 35 or those with other fertility issues, but it is more invasive and costly.

Anonymity and Confidentiality:

  • The identity of the sperm donor is kept confidential, and all personal information is protected.
  • These procedures are regulated under the Assisted Reproductive Technology (ART) Act, 2021 in India.
Conclusion

Donor sperm IVF offers a viable path to parenthood for many individuals and couples who face fertility challenges. With advances in reproductive medicine and robust legal frameworks, this procedure provides hope and new possibilities for building families.
