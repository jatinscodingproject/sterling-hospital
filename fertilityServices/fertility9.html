Recurrent Pregnancy Loss (RPL)

Advanced Fertility Treatment in Virar & Mumbai

Understanding Recurrent Pregnancy Loss

Recurrent Pregnancy Loss (RPL) is defined as having two or more consecutive miscarriages. It is a distressing condition that affects approximately 1% of couples trying to conceive. While occasional pregnancy loss is common and often random, recurrent losses may signal an underlying issue that needs medical attention and treatment.

Causes of Recurrent Pregnancy Loss
Genetic Factors:
  • Chromosomal Abnormalities: Genetic issues in either parent can lead to chromosomal abnormalities in the embryo, resulting in miscarriage.
  • Inherited Genetic Conditions: Certain genetic conditions passed down from parents can contribute to RPL.
Anatomical Factors:
  • Uterine Abnormalities: Structural issues such as uterine septum, fibroids, or scarring can interfere with embryo implantation and growth.
  • Cervical Insufficiency: A weak or short cervix can lead to pregnancy loss in the second trimester.
Hormonal and Metabolic Factors:
  • Thyroid Disorders: Both hyperthyroidism and hypothyroidism can increase the risk of miscarriage.
  • Diabetes: Uncontrolled diabetes can adversely affect pregnancy outcomes.
  • Polycystic Ovary Syndrome (PCOS): Women with PCOS are at a higher risk for RPL due to hormonal imbalances.
Immunological Factors:
  • Autoimmune Disorders: Conditions like antiphospholipid syndrome (APS) can lead to blood clots, affecting the placenta and causing miscarriage.
  • Alloimmune Factors: Incompatibilities between the mother's immune system and the embryo can result in pregnancy loss.
Infections:
  • Chronic Infections: Certain infections, including bacterial vaginosis, can increase the risk of miscarriage.
Environmental Factors:
  • Exposure to Toxins: Smoking, alcohol, and exposure to environmental toxins can contribute to RPL.
  • Lifestyle Factors: Stress, obesity, and poor diet may also play a role.
Diagnosis of Recurrent Pregnancy Loss
  • Medical History and Physical Examination: A thorough review of the patient's medical history and a physical examination.
  • Genetic Testing: Karyotyping of both partners and testing the products of conception for chromosomal abnormalities.
  • Imaging Studies: Ultrasound, hysteroscopy, or MRI to detect uterine abnormalities.
  • Hormonal and Metabolic Testing: Blood tests to evaluate thyroid function, glucose levels, and other hormonal factors.
  • Immunological Testing: Screening for autoimmune conditions and antiphospholipid syndrome.
  • Infectious Disease Screening: Tests to detect chronic infections that could contribute to RPL.
Treatment Options
  • Genetic Counseling and Testing: For couples with chromosomal abnormalities, genetic counseling can provide guidance. Preimplantation genetic testing (PGT) during IVF can help select embryos without genetic defects.
  • Surgical Interventions: Corrective surgery for uterine abnormalities like septum resection or removal of fibroids.
  • Hormonal Therapy: Managing thyroid disorders, diabetes, or progesterone supplementation to support early pregnancy.
  • Immunotherapy: Treatment for autoimmune conditions with medications like low-dose aspirin or heparin.
  • Lifestyle Modifications: Encouraging a healthy lifestyle, including quitting smoking, reducing alcohol intake, managing stress, and maintaining a healthy weight.
  • Supportive Care: Providing psychological support and counseling to help couples cope with the emotional impact of RPL.
