What is IVF?

In Vitro Fertilization (IVF) is an advanced assisted reproductive technology (ART) used to help individuals and couples conceive a child. The process involves retrieving eggs from a woman's ovaries, fertilizing them with sperm in a laboratory, and then transferring the resulting embryos into the woman's uterus. IVF bypasses certain infertility issues and enhances the chances of a successful pregnancy.

Indications for IVF

IVF is recommended for various fertility challenges, including:

  • Tubal Factor Infertility: Blocked or damaged fallopian tubes that prevent the egg and sperm from meeting.
  • Male Factor Infertility: Low sperm count, poor sperm motility, or abnormal sperm morphology.
  • Unexplained Infertility: No identifiable cause despite thorough evaluation.
  • Endometriosis: Severe cases that interfere with ovulation, fertilization, or implantation.
  • Ovulation Disorders: Conditions like Polycystic Ovary Syndrome (PCOS) and other hormonal disorders affecting ovulation.
  • Premature Ovarian Failure: Early menopause or significantly reduced ovarian function.
  • Genetic Disorders: Risk of passing on hereditary conditions, managed with Preimplantation Genetic Testing (PGT).
  • Previous Fertility Treatment Failures: Unsuccessful outcomes from fertility medications, intrauterine insemination (IUI), or surgery.
  • Age-Related Infertility: Advanced maternal age affecting egg quantity and quality.
  • Cancer Patients: Fertility preservation before undergoing treatments like chemotherapy or radiation.
  • Recurrent Pregnancy Loss: Multiple miscarriages that may benefit from genetic testing of embryos.

Our Comprehensive IVF Services

Initial Consultation and Assessment
  • Detailed Evaluation: Our fertility specialists conduct a thorough medical history review, physical examination, and diagnostic testing to identify the underlying causes of infertility.
  • Customized Treatment Plans: Based on your assessment, we create a personalized treatment plan designed to optimize your chances of success.
Advanced Diagnostic Testing
  • Hormonal Testing: Comprehensive hormone profiling to assess ovarian reserve and reproductive health.
  • Ultrasound Scans: High-resolution imaging to evaluate the health of reproductive organs.
  • Male Fertility Testing: Semen analysis and additional tests to assess male fertility factors.
IVF Treatment Cycle
  • Ovarian Stimulation: Medications are used to stimulate the ovaries to produce multiple eggs.
  • Egg Retrieval: A minor surgical procedure to collect mature eggs from the ovaries.
  • Sperm Collection: Collection of a sperm sample from the male partner or a donor.
  • Fertilization: Eggs and sperm are combined in the laboratory to facilitate fertilization.
  • Embryo Culture: Monitoring the development of fertilized eggs (embryos) in a controlled environment.
  • Embryo Transfer: The best-quality embryos are transferred into the uterus for implantation.
